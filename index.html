Since 2023, I am a tenure-track professor at the School of Artificial Intelligence, Jilin University. Fome 2020 to 2022, I was a Postdoc at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (2020.12-2022.12) and advised by Professor Hongyuan Zha, a X.Q. Deng Presidential Chair Professor of the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen and the Executive Dean of the School of Data Science. I received my Ph.D. from Xidian University in August,2020. My Ph.D. advisor was Professor Bo Chen. And I am also advised by Professor Mingyuan Zhou, an associate professor from the University of Texas at Austin.

My research lies at the intersection of statistical machine learning and its combinations with real-world applications. I am interested in probabilistic methods, deep generative models, representation learning and meta learning. These developed models and algorithms have been applied to time series modeling, text analysis, natural language processing, few-shot generation (classification), and automatic radar target recognition.

Research Highlights

  • [October, 2024] Our paper "Modality-Consistent Prompt Tuning with Optimal Transport" with Hairui Ren, and others will be published in IEEE TCSVT 2024. In this paper, we propose a novel prompt tuning strategy by distributionally matching different modalities.
  • [July, 2024] Our paper "Distribution alignment optimization through neural collapse for long-tailed classification" with Jintong Gao, He Zhao and Hongyuan Zha will be published in ICML 2024. In this paper, we develop a representation learning method by distribution alignment optimization through neural collapse.
  • [February, 2024] Our paper "PTaRL: Prototype-based tabular representation learning via space calibration" with Hangting Ye, and others will be published in ICLR 2024 (Spotlight) . In this paper, we develop a tabular representation learning framework via space calibration.
  • [September, 2023] Our paper "Enhancing Minority Classes by Mixing: An Adaptative Optimal Transport Approach for Long-tailed Classification" with Jintong Gao, Zhuo Li and He Zhao will be published in NeurIPS 2023 . In this paper, we develop a data augmentation method guided by optimal transport.
  • [May, 2023] Our paper "Suspicious Object Detection for Millimeter-Wave Images With Multi-View Fusion Siamese Network " with Long Tian, and others will be published in IEEE TIP 2023 . This paper introduces a Siamese-based detector and fuses the multi-view images for suspicious object detection in the MMW image dataset.
  • [September, 2022] Our paper "Adaptive Distribution Calibration for Few-Shot Learning with Hierarchical Optimal Transport " with Long Tian, He Zhao, Mingyuan Zhou and Hongyuan Zha will be published in NeurIPS 2022 . In this paper, we propose a novel distribution calibration method by learning the adaptive weight matrix between novel samples and base classes, which is built upon a hierarchical Optimal Transport (H-OT) framework.
  • [September, 2022] Our paper "Learning to Re-weight Examples with Optimal Transport for Imbalanced Classification " with Zhuo Li, Meixi Zheng, He Zhao, Mingyuan Zhou and Hongyuan Zha will be published in NeurIPS 2022 . This paper introduces a novel automatic re-weighting method for imbalance classification based on optimal transport (OT).
  • [May, 2022] Our paper "Matching Visual Features to Hierarchical Semantic Topics for Image Paragraph Captioning" with Ruiying Lu, Bo Chen and Mingyuan Zhou will be published in IJCV 2022. In this paper, we develop a plug-and-play hierarchical-topic-guided image paragraph generation pipeline, which couples a visual extractor with a deep topic model to guide the learning of a language paragraph generation model.

  • [February, 2022] Our paper "Learning Prototype-oriented Set Representations for Meta-Learning" with Long Tian, Minghe Zhang, Mingyuan Zhou and Hongyuan Zha will be published in ICLR 2022. In this paper, we present a novel OT-based method to improve existing summary networks designed for set-structured input based on optimal transport, where a set is endowed with two distributions: one is the empirical distribution over the data points, and another is the distribution over the learnable global prototypes. Our plug and play framework has shown appealing properties that can be applied to many meta-learning tasks.
